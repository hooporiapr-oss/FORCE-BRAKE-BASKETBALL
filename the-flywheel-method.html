The flywheel method

A training method built around output, control, and return.

Flywheel training creates resistance that responds to the athlete’s effort. The harder the output, the greater the demand to control the return. That makes the method especially relevant to basketball, where force production and force absorption are always linked.

Resistance that answers effort

Why it fits basketball

Basketball is constant output and constant absorption.

That is why this method feels sport-relevant. It does not only ask the athlete to create force. It asks the athlete to manage force, stop into shape, and access the next action with clarity.

Better movement is not only faster movement. It is more organized movement under force.

That is the heart of the flywheel method in basketball.
